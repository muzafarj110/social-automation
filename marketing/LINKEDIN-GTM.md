# Autopilot — LinkedIn Go-To-Market Pack

_Goal: turn LinkedIn into a customer-acquisition engine for the MVP. Budget: $0 ad spend — organic + founder time. Motion: posts + warm comment engagement._

## Strategy in one screen

- **Who:** lead for **solo founders** (fastest to convert, self-serve), but write so SMBs/agencies see themselves too.
- **Where:** your **personal profile is the engine** (posts, comments, DMs — best organic reach + the "I built this" story). The **Autopilot company page is the home** your posts link to and that reposts your content. Personal profiles out-reach brand pages ~10x — don't lead with the page.
- **Offer — Founding 50:** signup gives free starter credits (zero friction). The first **50 founding members** get a **lifetime 30% discount on every credit pack + 2× bonus credits on their first purchase + a "Founding Member" badge.** Scarcity (50 seats) is what turns a like into a signup.
- **CTA pattern:** ask for a **comment keyword** ("comment AUTOPILOT"), then DM the link. LinkedIn suppresses posts with outbound links, and comments boost reach — so never put the signup URL in the post body; put it in a comment and in DMs.
- **The dogfood hook:** every post is living proof — "this post was written and scheduled by the product I'm selling." That's your unfair advantage; use it relentlessly.

## The daily routine (≈45 min/day)

1. **Post** once per weekday (use Autopilot — approve-first — so you're dogfooding publicly).
2. **First 60 min after posting:** reply to every comment. Engagement velocity early = reach.
3. **20–30 min comment sprint:** leave thoughtful comments on 8–10 posts from your ICP and from larger creators in marketing/founder/SaaS spaces (see targeting below). This is how cold prospects discover you.
4. **Warm DMs only:** anyone who comments your keyword or engages 2+ times gets a personal DM (script below). Never cold-DM strangers — it burns the account.

## Targeting — who to engage with

Comment on and connect with people who match the ICP:
- Solo founders / indie hackers / "building in public" posters
- SMB owners posting about growth, hiring a marketer, or "no time to post"
- Agency owners and freelance marketers (your white-label resell angle)
- People complaining about: inconsistent posting, content burnout, "ChatGPT posts sound generic," social ROI

Engagement filter: their post is recent (<12h), the audience overlaps yours, and you can add a real insight (not "Great post!").

## Comment-engagement playbook

When you comment on a prospect's post, the goal is to be *useful and memorable*, never salesy:
- Add a specific insight or a counter-take that extends their point.
- Ask a sharp follow-up question (invites a reply → starts a relationship).
- Never pitch in a comment. The profile visit does the selling.
- 2–3 sentences max. One genuine idea beats a paragraph.

Rhythm: 8–10 quality comments/day on ICP + 2–3 larger creators in your space (their audience sees you).

## Warm-DM script (only for people who engaged)

**After they comment your keyword:**
> Hey [name] — thanks for the interest! Here's founding-member access: [link].
> Quick context: Autopilot runs your social like a marketing team — you set your voice once, it writes/tailors/schedules, you approve. Founding 50 get lifetime 30% off.
> Want me to point you at the 5-min setup, or happy to answer anything first.

**After they engage 2+ times (no keyword):**
> Hey [name], appreciated your take on [topic] the other day. I'm building Autopilot — an AI marketing team for founders — and opening 50 founding seats. Given what you posted about [their pain], thought it might be useful. Want the link? No pressure either way.

**Rules:** personalize the first line, never paste the same block cold, stop if they don't reply (one nudge max), keep it human.

## Measure weekly (what tells you it's working)

- Comments with your keyword (intent signal)
- Profile views + connection requests (reach working)
- Signups from DMs → **paid credit-pack purchases** (the only metric that matters)
- Which hook/post format drove the most keyword comments → make more of that

Kill/iterate signal: if 2 weeks of daily posting produces near-zero keyword comments, the *offer or ICP* is off, not the cadence — change the hook and the promise before changing frequency.
